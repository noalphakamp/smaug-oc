## Overview

Euractiv reporting on Poland preparing to amend its law on military deployments abroad to allow its forces to shoot down Russian objects, such as drones, over Ukraine without prior NATO or EU approval. This represents a significant shift in Poland's military engagement rules.

## Key Details

- **Proposed Change:** Amendment to allow Polish forces to engage Russian objects over Ukraine without NATO/EU approval
- **Target Objects:** Russian drones and other flying objects
- **Legal Context:** Changes to law on military deployments abroad
- **Rationale:** Enhanced air defense and rapid response capabilities

## Significance

This legal amendment would:
- Accelerate response times to Russian aerial threats
- Reduce bureaucratic approval requirements for defensive actions
- Potentially set precedent for other NATO/EU members
- Escalate Poland's direct involvement in countering Russian aerial threats

## Context

The proposed changes come amid ongoing Russian drone and missile attacks on Ukraine, with some objects potentially threatening Polish airspace or Ukrainian positions near the border. Poland has been one of Ukraine's strongest supporters since the conflict began.
